Neutrino Physics

Since October 2020, I have been part of the Harvard Neutrino group, where I have worked with Dr. Carlos Argüelles-Delgado and several other postdocs on projects including 'Simulating Neutrino Oscillations on a Quantum Processor' and 'Applying Machine Learning and a GNN to Neutrino Detection at IceCube'. Not only have I learned about scientific collaboration, but I have also developed a further interest in particle physics and research in general.

more will soon be available on github

Elementary Particles

For the past six months, I have been working with a group of Berkeley students at the Undergraduate Laboratory looking to understand and model elementary particle interactions and their respective Feynman diagrams using CERN's software ROOT and C++. We are also carrying out a literary review of Deepak Kar's textbook "Experimental Particle Physics: Understanding the measurements and searches at the LHC".
